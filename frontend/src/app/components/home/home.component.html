What is CBT?

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is a structured, short-term therapy that helps people identify and change negative patterns in their thoughts and behaviors. It's based on the idea that how we think affects how we feel and act.

CBT can help with a variety of issues such as anxiety, depression, stress, OCD, and PTSD. Instead of focusing on the past, it works on solving current problems by teaching practical skills.

  • Recognize negative or distorted thinking
  • Replace it with more realistic, helpful thoughts
  • Develop healthier responses and coping strategies

CBT usually lasts between 5 and 20 sessions with practical exercises to apply in everyday life. It’s one of the most effective and research-supported psychotherapies today.
